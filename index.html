Networked Beings

Tags: [3D Modelling] [Physical Computing] [Product]

Three networked objects; Bool, Boid, and Buzz explore how our interaction with objects alters our desire to interact with them. Through micro-interactions of turning each others light on they create a connection. Each object is created in a different form which relates directly to it’s output device. Bool is sharp outputting 1 or 0, and turning the affected device ...on or off. Boid is square, and has four outputs, each relating to a different colour state on the affected device. Buzz is circular and infinite, outputting an ever-changing random array of colours.

SLATE

Tags: [Branding] [Product]

This project is a collection of tools created for people with varying levels of additional needs to be able communicate more easily with their family, friends and guardians. The name SLATE is symbolic of taking each day as it comes, and offers the user a clean slate from which to communicate. A core aim is to produce tactile kinaesthetic experiences that are engaging and tactile. ...
The SLATE Daily Routine card deck is an initial exploration into the field of accessible design. It is the formalisation of a semiotic language that can be used to communicate routine by arranging activities in the order of your morning or evening routine. The SLATE Emotion Communication Cube further expands the scope for learning through the addition of physical computing elements. It is a visually engaging tool that, through the use of tactile touch sensitive buttons, gives the user the ability to communicate their emotions. The project at large continues to explore the many ways in which physical computing can be used to enable children and young adults with learning disabilities to be able to engage with the people around them, their learning and the world at large.

Santander Redbach 500

Tags: [Sportswear]

Cycling jersey design created in collaboration with Kalas Sportswear for 25 Cyclists of varying abilities riding from Redhill to Mönchengladbach over 3 days - bringing two offices & two countries together. Inspired by the cobblestones that would face the riders on their 500 kilometre trip, the jersey features a cobblestone pattern detailing the 5 different countries that they would visit.

London to Paris

Tags: [Sportswear]

A set of 4 jerseys designed for each day of the Cure Leukemia charity bike ride from London to Paris. The design takes inspiration from the finish town for each day, subtly guiding the riders towards their destination and acting as a piece of memorabilia. Created in collaboration with Kalas Sportswear, the jersey also denotes the day of the trip through the classic 6-day inspired numbers on the riders shoulders.

The Tragic Everyday

Tags: [Animation] [3D Model]

The tragic everyday explores the linear nature to our lives, passing from womb on a direct path to an office desk - and back again. Asking questions around how engaging with the ‘rat race’ of life conditions us to pass things by and not stop to pause and what we’ve achieved up to this point.
